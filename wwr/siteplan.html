Content

Home page

Site Introduction: The home page of Ethan Portfolio explains what visitors can expect to find and why the site exists. It serves as a professional introduction for anyone who wants a quick, organized look at my work as a developing web designer and programmer. Instead of making a visitor search through class portals, file folders, or separate project links, the home page brings everything into one place. It introduces my focus on clean design, practical problem solving, and steady improvement as I build skills in HTML, CSS, layout, accessibility, and project organization.

Visitor Questions: People who arrive on the site will usually want answers to a few simple questions right away: who is Ethan James, what kind of work does he do, and where should I click next? The home page will answer those questions with a short introduction, a preview of my strongest skills, and clear links to the About Me and Projects pages. That structure matters because my audience includes instructors, classmates, internship coordinators, and employers who may only spend a minute or two on the site before deciding whether they want to keep reading.

First Impression: I want the home page to feel professional, approachable, and easy to scan. The text will highlight the kind of work I enjoy, the tools I am learning, and the value of seeing both my process and final results. A strong home page should not just say that I build websites; it should show that I care about communication, consistency, and thoughtful design. This page will act as the front door of the site and guide visitors toward the sections that fit their interests.

About Me

Background: The About Me page gives visitors a fuller picture of who I am beyond a single headline. It will explain my current studies, the kinds of assignments and projects that have helped me grow, and the habits that shape how I work. This page is important because a portfolio is stronger when it includes the person behind the projects. Visitors should leave this page with a sense of my reliability, curiosity, and willingness to learn through practice, revision, and feedback.

Skills and Work Style: I plan to use this section to describe the technical and personal strengths that support my work. That includes writing semantic HTML, organizing CSS carefully, following instructions closely, and improving projects through testing and refinement. I also want this page to show that I value communication and consistency, because those qualities matter in both school and professional settings. Visitors who read this page should understand that I am still growing, but that I am serious about building useful, polished work.

Goals: Another purpose of the About Me page is to show direction. I want visitors to know that this site is not only a record of what I have already completed, but also a place that will continue to grow as I add stronger projects and deeper skills. By including my goals, interests, and development process, the page can help employers or instructors understand the kind of opportunities I am preparing for.

Images for the About Me page

Projects

Project Selection: The Projects page will focus on examples of work that show what I can build and how I think through a problem. Rather than listing every assignment without explanation, this page will feature a smaller number of projects with enough description to make each one meaningful. Visitors should be able to see what the project was, what tools I used, what challenge I solved, and what I learned from the process.

What the Work Shows: Each project summary will help answer practical questions that a visitor might have. Can I create clear layouts? Can I organize content so that it is easy to read? Can I follow a design plan and still make thoughtful choices? The Projects page is where those answers become visible. I want to include short descriptions of responsibilities, screenshots, and notes about revisions so the page communicates both final results and the thinking behind them.

Call to Action: This page will also encourage visitors to keep exploring. A strong projects section can lead a classmate to ask questions, an instructor to see improvement over time, or a potential employer to contact me for more details. For that reason, the page should feel organized, current, and focused on quality over quantity. It will show that my portfolio is more than a class requirement; it is a record of growth and a place where my best work can be reviewed quickly.

Wireframes

Three wireframes are included below, one for each page of the site. Each wireframe uses the same shared header with logo and navigation, plus a footer, while changing the main content to fit the page purpose.

Home

The home wireframe emphasizes a short introduction near the top, followed by skill highlights and quick previews of the About Me and Projects pages. It includes a hero image, headline, supporting text, and the shared header and footer structure.

About Me

The About Me wireframe places personal background and strengths first, then uses supporting images and a short goals section to keep the page balanced. It keeps the same site header and footer while adding clear image and text areas for biography, strengths, and goals.

Projects

The Projects wireframe is built around project cards with screenshots, short summaries, and space for key takeaways from each featured build. The layout also includes the shared logo, navigation, and footer so the page stays consistent with the rest of the site.
